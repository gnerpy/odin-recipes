Pizza Dough Recipe

Home

Description

Made with 4 ingredients — flour, water, salt, and yeast — this simple, no-knead pizza dough recipe can be ready start to finish in 3 hours. It produces a pizza with a ballooned edge and crisp but pliable crust. You can use the dough the same day you mix it or store it in the fridge for up to 5 days (or freeze it!).

Ingredients

  • 4 cups (512 g) bread flour or all-purpose flour, plus more for assembly
  • 3 to 4 teaspoons (10 to 15 g) kosher salt
  • 1 teaspoon (4 g) instant yeast
  • 1.75 to 2 cups (400 to 454 g) lukewarm water

Cooking Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, salt, and instant yeast. Add the water and, using a rubber spatula, mix until the water is absorbed and the ingredients form a sticky dough ball.
  2. Pour a drop or two of oil over the top and rub with your hands to coat. Cover the bowl with a damp tea towel or plastic wrap and set aside in a warm spot to rise for 1 to 1 1/2 hours, or until the dough has doubled in bulk.
  3. Optional: If time permits, 30 minutes after covering the bowl, perform one set of stretches and folds (stretch the dough up and in 8 to 10 times) to strengthen the dough.
  4. Place a Baking Steel or pizza stone in the top third of the oven and preheat to its hottest setting (550°F). Ensure the steel heats for at least 45 minutes once the oven reaches temperature.
  5. Liberally flour a work surface and turn the dough out. Use a bench scraper to divide the dough into 4 equal portions. With floured hands, roll each portion into a ball by pinching the dough underneath.
  6. Proof the dough: If baking immediately, let the balls sit on their tucked-in edges for 30 to 60 minutes in a lidded, lightly floured vessel. If using refrigerated dough, remove it from the fridge 60 to 90 minutes before baking to proof.
  7. Handling the dough minimally, shape it into a 10″–12″ round using the backs of your hands.
  8. Lay a sheet of parchment paper on a pizza peel (optionally using a few drops of oil on the paper) and transfer the dough round to the peel.
  9. Top the pizza:
    • For Margherita: Spread 2 oz tomato sauce, 3 oz mozzarella, drizzle with olive oil, and add a pinch of flaky sea salt.
    • For Kale & Crème Fraîche: Spread 1–2 tbsp crème fraîche, sprinkle with garlic and grated Parmigiano Reggiano, and top with kale (tossed in olive oil and salt).
  10. Shimmy the pizza (parchment and all) into the oven and bake for about 5 minutes until the top is blistered.
  11. Transfer to a cutting board, discard the parchment paper, garnish with basil (if making Margherita), cut, and serve.
